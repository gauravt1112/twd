FAQ'S
Below you will find answers to the most frequently asked questions about Times Women's Drive. If you do not find the answers you are looking for, kindly get in touch with us at support@timeswomensdrive.com

What is the destination of the Times Women’s Drive?

The Times Women’s Drive is headed to Goa this year.

What is the TSD format of rally?

Please refer to the “Rules and Regulations” section for more info.

What is a Tulip Chart?

A Tulip Chart is a Pictorial representation of navigation instructions to be followed in a rally. It includes details like direction, speed, distance and is the only navigation tool to be used for the rally.

How many days is the rally for?

The rally goes on for 2 days. (i.e, 5th and 6th March), with a night halt at the end of Day.

How will the winner be chosen?

Each city route will be treated as a separate rally and winners will be selected from each city for their respective rally. The basis of selection of the winners will be according to the TSD formula and Rules and Regulations. Please refer the “Rules and Regulations” section for more info.

What date and time is flag off and from where?

The date of the flag off is 5th March and time is approx. 6am from each city. The exact address of the Flag Off will be shared progressively.

What is the duration of the drive?

Since the Times Women’s Drive is a Time-Speed-Distance (TSD) rally and not a race, the Drive will take place over 2 days starting 5th March. Ideally, it will start at approx. 6am on 5th March and culminate at approx. 5pm on 6th March. The participants have to complete the drive as per distance and speed given in the roadbook. However, the time taken by the participants of the drive depends on how well the team navigates the route and how closely they follow the Tulip Chart. Please refer to the “What You Should Know” section for more info.

How do I register?

Please refer to the “ What You Should Know” section for more info.

Who does the participating team comprise?

The team consists of minimum of 1 Driver and 1 Navigator. A maximum of 1 additional passenger or the Team Correspondent can join. Keep in mind only if you have a Team Correspondent will you be able to participate in the social media contests and activities we have during the drive.

What are the criteria for participation?

As long as you are a female, have a valid driver’s license and know how to drive, you may participate. The participating women must be in the age band of 18 to 55 years.

If I live in another city besides Mumbai, Pune or Bangalore, can I still participate in the rally?

The rally is open to all women residing in the country.

Does every participant have to be from the same city?

Every participant need not be from the same city.

Can a person who is not an Indian Citizen, participate in the Times Women’s Drive?

Yes, but only as the Team Correspondent, and cannot take part as Driver or Navigator. We are happy to see your foreign national friends participate with you on this adventurous drive.

What are the legal documents required to participate in the rally?

Follow this link to know it all What you should know

Are the documents of all 3 participants to be submitted or just those of the driver?

The vehicle related documents like PUC, Registration, etc will be singular for the vehicle, the driver’s licence copies will be required for just the drivers who will be driving throughout, but all other documents will be required for each participant. Please refer the “After Being Selected” section under the What You Should Know tab for more info.

Do we submit our documents online or offline?

You are required to upload the documents on our website as well as submit them in person. We will notify all participants of the submission location through special emailers. Please refer the “After Being Selected” section under the What You Should Know tab for more info.

Is the participation cost INR 10000 per person?

The participation fee for the Times Women's Drive is INR 10,000 per team, not per person.
Please refer to Fees And Inclusions section for more information.

Do I have to bring my own car or will it be provided by Times Women’s Drive?

Each team must have a car to participate in Times Women’s Drive. If the car is not owned by the Driver or Navigator, authorization letter from the owner will be required. Please refer to the “ What You Should Know” section for more info.

What is not included in the participation fees?

To know all the exciting inclusions follow Fees And Inclusions

What is the last date for registration?

You don't want to be left behind. So hurry! Registrations close on the 15th of February.

Will I get a receipt / confirmation message after I make the online transaction?

Yes, you will get a receipt and conformation via email after making the transaction.

Will accommodation be provided in Goa by Times Women’s Drive?

No. Times Women’s Drive will only provide dinner and stay for the night at the end of Leg 1 of the rally

Can my team be changed after registering?

No. However under a valid reason solely at the discretion of the Times Women’s Drive team, the change may be allowed before 15th Feb, subject to all eligibility criteria being met adequately.

Are the registrations transferrable?

TIMES WOMEN’S DRIVE registrations are not transferrable.

If I get selected and choose to withdraw from the rally, will I get a refund?

In the case that the participant chooses to withdraw from the rally, Times Women’s Drive will honour a refund, provided participants have informed the Times team of the same, before the closing date of registration. After this no amount will be refunded.

Can I register multiple times using different numbers and email ids?

No, multiple and duplicate entries will not be considered by the Times Women’s Drive panel.

Can I bring my family along?

The rally and its events are exclusive to the participants of the Times Women’s Drive.

Is there a speed limit?

Yes. The rally works on a TSD format and all road speed limits will have to be adhered to. Find all you need to know at What you should know.

How will we navigate?

Please refer to the “Drive Route Details” section under “ What You Should Know”.

Do all 3 participants have to be present for the briefing?

If you are going to be driving or are the navigator, it is really important for you to be at the briefing. You don't want to miss out on any key details and important instructions. However, we encourage the 3rd participant (Team Correspondent) to be present for the briefing as well.

Why do I have to attend the briefing?

The briefing session is a compulsory part of a TSD rally that every participant has to attend. The latest details about the drive, the rules and regulations, and queries will be discussed at the briefing. Alongside participants will also be exposed to various offers from our partners and will get to interact with them along with food and beverages. Please refer to the “ What You Should Know” section for more info). If you do not attend the briefing, then you may be excluded or a penalty amount will be payable by you.

Can someone else attend the briefing for me?

No. There’s a lot to know at the briefing, and you do not want to miss out on essential information for the drive. It is important for you to attend the briefing in person and no one else can attend it for you.

How and when will we know if I have been shortlisted to participate in the rally?

Once selected, every qualifying participant will be sent an email from Times Women’s Drive, informing them of all the forms to be duly filled and submitted to support@timeswomensdrive.com.

Can I campaign for votes?

Go all out. Let your loved ones help you win.

Can a voter vote multiple times for one participant?

No, a voter can cast only one single vote for a particular participant.

Can voters vote offline as well?

Voters must vote using our online platforms.

Are we allowed to carry cell phones and other devices during the rally?

What’s the fun in an amazing drive if you can’t capture memories? Make sure you carry your power banks and fully charged phones along. For safety, you are not allowed to talk or chat or take pictures if you are driving and other participants have to take care while using the phone so as not to distract the driver or navigator.

Can we halt midway to rest?

There will be pit time controls or passage controls at undisclosed locations en route.

Is it compulsory to decorate my car for the rally?

It isn’t compulsory, but highly encouraged. Utmost precautions to be taken to ensure that no items used for decoration blocks the view of the driver or navigator and that no items should block parts such as radiators, grills or AC vents or interfere with the safe driving of the vehicle.

Will we get material from Times Women’s Drive to decorate our car?

No. The participants will have to use their own materials for decoration. They will only be given competition related stickers for their cars.

Do all the competitors start the rally at once?

The starting order will be determined by the organizers after all valid entries are accepted. Each vehicle will be flagged off at 1 minute intervals.

Is there any medical facility in case of any emergency?

Medical support will be accessible at all times during the drive.

How do I get the two-day rally insurance?

Your usual vehicle insurance policy does NOT cover the two day insurance for rallies. Please contact your insurance company to obtain a special rally Insurance Cover for the two day rally on 5th and 6th March 2016. Assistance on this will be provided if required by sending query on support@timeswomensdrive.com
